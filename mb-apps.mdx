## Overview

MB App Store lets you add focused apps to your ModelBoard account so you can extend profiles, manage workflows, and connect to external tools without custom development.

ModelBoard treats MB Apps as one part of a broader integrations layer that also includes external services, directories, and webhooks. MB Apps run inside the ModelBoard environment, while integrations handle data exchange between ModelBoard and other platforms.

<Callout kind="info">

MB Apps availability and layout can vary based on your account type and region. If you do not see the MB App Store in your navigation, contact support or your organization owner.

</Callout>

## How MB Apps relate to integrations

Integrations connect ModelBoard data to external systems. MB Apps are one type of integration that installs directly into your ModelBoard account.

- Use **MB Apps** when you want in-product tools that live alongside your boards, profiles, and projects.
- Use **external integrations** when you need ModelBoard to sync or notify other systems such as CRMs, directories, or custom endpoints.

MB Apps often rely on the same underlying integrations layer as directories and webhooks, so your data can move consistently across tools.

<Callout kind="tip">

Review the broader integrations capabilities in [Integrations](/integrations) when planning how apps and external systems should work together.

</Callout>

## Who MB Apps are for

Use MB Apps differently depending on whether you are working as a model, business, studio, or enterprise owner.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Model accounts" icon="user">

Models use MB Apps to enhance individual profiles and streamline personal workflows.

- Highlight additional media or credits on your profile.
- Track inquiries and bookings initiated from partner apps.
- Manage personal documentation related to shoots or collaborations.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Business accounts" icon="briefcase">

Businesses adopt MB Apps to support client-facing workflows and coordination with collaborators.

- Organize client projects alongside model profiles.
- Share documentation and briefs with partners through connected apps.
- Coordinate outreach and follow-up across multiple boards.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Studio accounts" icon="monitor">

Studios integrate MB Apps into production planning and on-set workflows.

- Manage call sheets, schedules, and roles tied to each shoot.
- Coordinate crew and talent information in one place.
- Attach compliance checks or documentation workflows to production timelines.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Enterprise accounts" icon="building-2">

Enterprise accounts use MB Apps to keep workflows consistent across multiple entities.

- Standardize which apps teams may use across brands or subsidiaries.
- Ensure data from apps and integrations flows into internal reporting or compliance systems.
- Govern app access centrally while allowing teams to choose tools within policy.

</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Browse and discover apps

Find MB Apps from within your ModelBoard account and evaluate how they fit your workflow.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the MB App Store" icon="layers">

  - Sign in to your ModelBoard account.
  - Open the navigation menu and select the section labeled MB Apps or App Store.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter and search" icon="search">

  - Use search or filters (such as category or account type) to narrow the list.
  - Open an app detail page to see its description, capabilities, and required permissions.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review compatibility" icon="info">

  - Confirm the app supports your account type and any required prerequisites.
  - Check whether the app depends on specific integrations or data access you have enabled.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Install an MB App

Install apps from their detail pages and confirm what access they will have.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app detail page" icon="file-text">

  - From the MB App Store, select an app to open its detail page.
  - Review the overview, supported account types, and any usage notes.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Review permissions" icon="shield">

  - Read the list of data and actions the app requests, such as profile access, board access, or messaging.
  - Confirm the app aligns with your internal policies, especially if you manage other people’s data.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize and install" icon="check-circle">

  - Select the install or add button to start installation.
  - Complete any authorization prompts, such as choosing which boards, profiles, or teams the app may access.
  - Wait for the confirmation message that the app is installed and ready.

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

If you work in a Business, Studio, or Enterprise account, check whether your organization owner has policies for which apps may be installed or who must approve them.

</Callout>

## Manage installed apps

Adjust permissions, usage, or remove apps from your account as your workflows change.

<Steps>
  <Step title="View installed apps" icon="settings">

  - Open your account or organization settings.
  - Locate the section that lists installed apps or connected tools.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Update settings and access" icon="edit">

  - Select an app to view its settings.
  - Adjust which boards, profiles, or teams the app may access, if the app supports scoped access.
  - Save your changes, then confirm the app still behaves as expected in your workflows.

  </Step>
  <Step title="Uninstall or revoke access" icon="trash">

  - When you no longer need an app, choose the option to uninstall or disconnect it.
  - Confirm the removal and review whether the app retains any historical data outside ModelBoard.
  - Notify affected collaborators if uninstalling the app will change their access or workflows.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Security, permissions, and compliance

Understanding how MB Apps interact with your ModelBoard data helps you choose and manage them safely.

- MB Apps may request read or write access to profiles, boards, messages, or other data types.
- Some apps operate only on data inside ModelBoard; others may sync information to or from external systems.
- Organization owners in Business, Studio, and Enterprise accounts typically control which users may install or configure apps.

<Callout kind="alert">

ModelBoard does not replace your legal obligations. Use 2257 and compliance tooling to support record-keeping, but always confirm with your own legal counsel that your processes meet jurisdictional requirements.

</Callout>

When you install or manage an app, review its permissions carefully and coordinate with your internal security or legal teams if you handle sensitive or regulated data.
