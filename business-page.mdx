## Understand business listings and pages

Business listings give your organization visibility in the public Business Directory and link through to a detailed Business Page.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="For visitors" icon="users">

Visitors discover and evaluate businesses through two main surfaces:

- **Business Directory** at `/business-directory`
- **Business Page** at `/business/:id` (one page per business)

</Tab>

<Tab title="For business owners" icon="briefcase">

As an organization owner, you manage a single business profile that powers both:

- The **card** shown in the directory
- The **full profile** shown on your Business Page

You edit this profile from **Business Management** at `/business-management`.

</Tab>
</Tabs>

### Business Directory (`/business-directory`)

The Business Directory at `/business-directory` shows a grid of business cards with filters and search so visitors can find relevant providers.

Each card typically includes:

- Banner, logo, or avatar
- Business name
- Category badge
- Location
- Short description snippet
- Service and product counts
- Optional badges such as featured, sponsored, or verified

When a visitor clicks a card, they go to the corresponding Business Page at `/business/:id`.

### Business Page (`/business/:id`)

The Business Page at `/business/:id` is the detailed profile for your organization.

The page layout includes:

- A hero banner with your logo or avatar
- Business name and primary category badge
- Verified badge (when applicable)
- Tabs to organize content:
  - **About**: description, categories, and location
  - **Contact**: address, phone, website, contact email, social media
  - **Services**: list of services you offer
  - **Products**: list of products you offer

## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info">

To create and publish a business listing, you need:

- An **Organization Owner** account
- A **Business account** with access to the Business Platform
- Permission to represent the organization and publish its details

You manage all business data from **Business Management** at `/business-management`.

</Callout>

## Create and publish your business listing

Follow this flow once to get your business discoverable in the directory.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register or confirm your business account" icon="check-circle">

Start by confirming that you are using an Organization Owner account with access to the Business Platform.

- Sign in and navigate to the Business Platform area.
- Confirm that you see **Business Management** at `/business-management`.
- If needed, complete any initial business onboarding required by your plan.

Success looks like: you can open `/business-management` and see your business profile settings.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill out your business profile" icon="edit">

Next, complete the core profile information that powers both the directory card and your Business Page.

- Open `/business-management`.
- Enter your **business name** and select at least one **category**.
- Add a **description**, **location**, and **contact information**.
- Upload your **logo/avatar** and **banner** images.

Success looks like: your profile shows a clear name, category, description, location, and visuals without placeholder data.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Add services and products" icon="package">

Add what you actually offer so visitors can understand and compare your business.

- In Business Management, open the **Services** section and add your key services with descriptions and prices.
- Open the **Products** section and list any products, again with clear descriptions and prices.
- Respect any visible service or product limits based on your subscription.

Success looks like: the Services and Products sections each show at least one well-described item.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Review visibility and moderation status" icon="info">

Before your listing appears publicly, it may go through moderation.

- Check the **moderation status** of your business profile.
- Confirm that your profile content complies with any content and quality guidelines.
- Update any fields that could cause confusion or rejection.

Success looks like: your moderation status is set to `approved` and not `pending` or `rejected`.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Publish and verify appearance in the directory" icon="play-circle">

Once approved, your business will appear in the public directory.

- Open `/business-directory`.
- Use search, category, or location filters to find your business card.
- Click your card to confirm that the Business Page at `/business/:id` loads correctly.

Success looks like: your business appears in search and filters, and all tabs on your Business Page display your content correctly.

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="alert">

If your business does not appear in `/business-directory`, first confirm that your moderation status is `approved`. If the status is `pending` or `rejected`, update your profile and follow any guidance provided in Business Management.

</Callout>

## Business listing fields and how they appear

Your business listing is stored as a business profile and exposed across the directory and Business Page.

### Core profile fields

<ParamField body="business_name" param-type="string" required="true" show-location="false">

Public business name shown prominently:

- As the main title on the Business Page hero
- On the Business Directory card

Use the exact name visitors expect to search for.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="category" param-type="enum" required="true" show-location="false">

Primary business category (for example, `agency`, `marketing_pr`):

- Shown as a **category badge** near your name on both the Business Directory card and Business Page
- Used for category filtering in the directory

Choose the category that best matches your main business activity.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="categories" param-type="string[]" show-location="false">

Optional additional categories:

- May be shown on the About tab to describe your focus areas
- Can improve how well visitors understand your specialization

Add only relevant categories to avoid confusing visitors.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="description" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Short to medium description of your business:

- A shortened version appears as the **description snippet** on the directory card
- The full text appears on the **About** tab of your Business Page

Focus on what you do, who you serve, and what makes you a good fit.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="location" param-type="string" show-location="false">

High-level location (for example, city and country):

- Displayed on the Business Directory card
- Appears on the About or Contact sections on the Business Page

Use a format visitors recognize and that matches how they filter in the directory.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="address" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Detailed street address:

- Shown in the **Contact** tab on the Business Page
- Not always shown on the directory card depending on layout

Include this if in-person visits or a physical office matter to your business.

</ParamField>

### Contact and online presence

<ParamField body="phone" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Public phone number:

- Shown on the **Contact** tab on the Business Page
- May appear as a quick-contact item in some layouts

Use a number that is actively monitored during your business hours.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="website" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Primary website URL:

- Displayed on the **Contact** tab on the Business Page
- Often a key entry point for visitors who want to learn more or convert

Link to a page that speaks directly to the audience you expect from the directory.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="contact_email" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Public contact email:

- Listed in the **Contact** tab
- Used by visitors to send inquiries from outside the platform

Use a shared inbox or role-based email so multiple team members can respond.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="social_media" param-type="map" show-location="false">

Map of social media profiles (for example, keys for `linkedin`, `twitter`, `instagram`):

- Links appear on the **Contact** or **About** tab of your Business Page
- Help visitors validate your presence and see recent activity

Include profiles that are active and aligned with your brand.

</ParamField>

### Images and branding

<ParamField body="avatar_url" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Avatar or small logo image:

- Shown in the Business Page hero beside your name
- Often appears on the Business Directory card

Use a clear, square image that is readable at small sizes.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="logo_url" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Primary logo image (if different from avatar):

- May be used on larger displays of your brand
- Supports more complex branding than a simple avatar

Prefer a logo with good contrast against both light and dark backgrounds.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="banner_url" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Hero banner image:

- Displayed as the background at the top of your Business Page
- Sets visual context for your brand

Avoid heavy text in the banner; keep it visually clean so your name and badges stay readable.

</ParamField>

### Services

Services define what you deliver and how, and they appear in the **Services** tab.

<ParamField body="services" param-type="ServiceItem[]" show-location="false">

Array of services you offer:

- Each service appears as a card or row under the **Services** tab on your Business Page
- The total count may appear on your Business Directory card

Respect any `service_limit` associated with your subscription tier.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].name" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Service name:

- Shown as the title of each service entry in the Services tab
- Used by visitors to scan and compare offerings quickly

Make names specific and outcome-focused, not generic.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].description" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Service description:

- Appears under the service name on your Business Page
- Explains what is included and who it is for

Keep this concise but concrete (scope, format, and main value).

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].price" param-type="string|number" show-location="false">

Service price:

- Displayed alongside each service in the **Services** tab
- Helps filter out misaligned leads before they contact you

Use a currency and format that matches your primary market.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].features" param-type="string[]" show-location="false">

List of key service features:

- Often displayed as bullet points in the service detail
- Highlights inclusions and differentiators

Use features to clarify scope and avoid misunderstandings.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].picture" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Optional service image:

- Shown in the service card where supported
- Gives visual context for the service

Prefer simple visuals that align with your overall branding.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].processing_time" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Expected duration or delivery time for the service:

- Displayed in service details on your Business Page
- Helps set expectations up front

Use concrete ranges (for example, "5–7 business days") instead of vague terms.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].extras" param-type="string[]" show-location="false">

Optional add-ons or extras for the service:

- Shown in service details where supported
- Encourages visitors to consider higher-value packages

Describe extras clearly so visitors know what they are paying for.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].warranty" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Warranty or guarantee details:

- Displayed as part of the service description
- Builds trust and clarifies what happens if expectations are not met

Keep this aligned with your actual terms.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="services[].revision_rounds" param-type="number" show-location="false">

Number of included revision rounds (for creative or iterative work):

- Appears in the service details
- Prevents disagreements later by setting clear limits

Use an integer that reflects your standard process.

</ParamField>

### Products

Products appear in the **Products** tab of your Business Page.

<ParamField body="products" param-type="ProductItem[]" show-location="false">

Array of products you offer:

- Each product appears as a card or row under the **Products** tab
- The total count may be referenced on your Business Directory card

Subscription tiers may enforce a `product_limit`.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="products[].name" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Product name:

- Shown as the title of each product entry
- Used by visitors to quickly scan your catalog

Choose names that are recognizable and descriptive.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="products[].description" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Product description:

- Appears under the product name
- Explains what the product is and when to use it

Focus on benefits and key specs, not internal jargon.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="products[].price" param-type="string|number" show-location="false">

Product price:

- Displayed with each product in the **Products** tab
- Helps visitors self-qualify before contacting you

Use your standard price or a clear starting price.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="products[].image" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Product image:

- Shown in the product card
- Improves scanning and recognition

Use high-quality images that accurately represent the product.

</ParamField>

## Moderation, verification, and visibility

Visibility in the directory depends on moderation status and flags on your business profile.

### Moderation status

<ParamField body="moderation_status" param-type="enum" show-location="false">

Overall review status of your business profile. Typical values:

- `pending`: your listing is submitted but not yet approved; it may not be visible in `/business-directory`.
- `approved`: your listing passed review and appears in the directory and on your Business Page.
- `rejected`: your listing did not meet requirements; you may need to update content before resubmitting.

Check and manage this status from Business Management.

</ParamField>

<Callout kind="info">

If your moderation status is `rejected`, look for any messages or guidelines in Business Management, adjust your profile content, and resubmit. Focus on clear, accurate descriptions and compliant content.

</Callout>

### Verification and featured placement

<ParamField body="verified" param-type="boolean" show-location="false">

Indicates whether your business has been verified:

- When true, shows a **verified badge** next to your business name on the Business Page
- May also show on the Business Directory card

Verification helps visitors trust that the business is legitimate and controlled by an authorized owner.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="featured" param-type="boolean" show-location="false">

Indicates whether your business is marked as featured:

- Featured businesses can receive prominent placement in `/business-directory`
- Some layouts may label these cards as **featured** or **sponsored**

Featured status is usually controlled by your plan, promotions, or platform settings.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="service_limit" param-type="number" show-location="false">

Maximum number of services you can publish:

- Enforced in Business Management when adding services
- Tied to your current subscription tier

Prioritize your most important or highest-converting services if you hit this limit.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="product_limit" param-type="number" show-location="false">

Maximum number of products you can publish:

- Enforced in Business Management when adding products
- Also tied to your subscription tier

List only the products that best represent your catalog for new visitors.

</ParamField>

## Manage staff for your business

Staff accounts let multiple people help manage your business profile without sharing one login.

All staff management happens inside **Business Management** at `/business-management`. Staff details are **not exposed** on the public Business Page unless you explicitly add team information elsewhere (for example, in the About description).

<ParamField body="business_staff" param-type="object[]" show-location="false">

Staff members linked to your business:

- Managed only in Business Management
- Used to grant access to edit your profile, services, products, analytics, and settings

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="business_staff[].job_title" param-type="string" show-location="false">

Role or job title for the staff member:

- Helps you understand who is responsible for what internally
- Not automatically shown on your public Business Page

Use real roles (for example, "Marketing Manager", "Sales Lead").

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="business_staff[].permissions" param-type="string[]" show-location="false">

List of permission flags for the staff member:

- Controls what they can do in Business Management (for example, edit profile, manage services, view analytics)
- Lets you delegate specific duties without granting full owner access

Assign only the permissions needed for each role.

</ParamField>

<ParamField body="business_staff[].active" param-type="boolean" show-location="false">

Whether the staff member's access is currently active:

- Active staff can sign in and manage the business based on their permissions
- Inactive staff keep their record but lose access

Set `active` to false when someone leaves or no longer needs access.

</ParamField>

<Callout kind="tip">

Review staff permissions regularly so only the right people can change your business profile, services, and products. This reduces the risk of incorrect public information.

</Callout>

## Tips to improve discovery and conversion

Use these practical adjustments to help more visitors find and choose your business.

1. **Align categories with your real focus**  
   Pick the primary category that best matches your main service line, and use additional categories sparingly to avoid diluting your positioning.

2. **Write a focused, visitor-first description**  
   Lead with who you help and what outcomes you deliver. Avoid internal jargon and generic claims; show specific expertise and typical client scenarios.

3. **Complete your Services and Products tabs**  
   Add at least 3–5 of your most requested services or products, each with a clear description and price. This lets visitors self-qualify before contacting you.

4. **Use high-quality, consistent images**  
   Upload a readable logo/avatar and a clean banner. For services and products, use images that match your brand style and make it obvious what is being offered.

5. **Keep contact information current and simple**  
   Provide one primary email, one phone number, and a website that leads directly to relevant information or an inquiry form. Test that all links and phone numbers work.

6. **Activate only relevant social media links**  
   Link to profiles where you are active and responsive. Inactive or outdated profiles can hurt trust and conversion.

7. **Maintain accurate location details**  
   Use a city and region that match how your target customers search. If you operate remotely, make that clear in your description while still setting a base location.

8. **Monitor moderation status and iterate**  
   If your listing is pending or rejected, adjust your content based on the platform's expectations. Cleaner, more specific descriptions usually perform better and are easier to approve.
